Trash the Ash: The Dangers of Tobacco

Tobacco is one of the most widely used recreational drugs worldwide, and experts predict the number of users to increase drastically. With over 1.1 BILLION tobacco users worldwide, that number is expected to increase to 1.6 BILLION over the course of the next 20 years. In the U.S. alone, the average tobacco user spends $2,000 annually on tobacco products. With such a large volume of users, it was reported that tobacco industries made approximentally $744 BILLION in 2014.

Nicotine: The Dangers of Addiction

Nicotine is a stimulant created by tobacco plants. Known to be one of the most addictive chemicals on the planet, it can be very hard for users to quit using tobacco products because of their body's addiction to the drug. Tobacco products are created in many forms, which can be seen in the table below.

Product Description
Cigarettes Users inhale the tobacco smoke. Most used product worldwide
Chewing Tobacco Users chew on pieces in the corner of their mouth. Nicotine enters through mouth tissue
Dipping Tobacco Users store between their gums and spit product waste
Cigars Cigar smoke is also inhaled through the longs. Often used on special occasions
Snuff Inhaled through the nasal cavity. Can develop chronic nose problems
Quit Smoking Before Smoking Quits You!

Tobacco smoke contains over 4,000 chemicals, and many chemicals are created once tobacco is ignited. Chemicals including ammonia, also used in household cleaning products, is added into cigarettes to maximize nictotine absorption. Common side effects of tobacco use include but are not limited to:

  • Mouth, larynx, esophagus, kidney, liver, bladder, pancreas, stomach, and colon cancer
  • COPD
  • Chronic bronchitis
  • Emphysema
  • Seizures
  • Respiratory arrest
  • Heart disease
  • tuberculosis
  • erectile disfunction

Heart disease and cancer are the 1st and 2nd leading causes of death in the United States

Is it worth your life?

One person dies every SIX SECONDS from tobacco related diseases, with over twenty percent of deaths in the U.S. caused by tobacco. In addition, nearly 600,000 people who are non-smokers die annually from secondhand smoke.

It's not too late to quit. Choosing to stop using tobacco products can have almost immedite benefits to your health. If you have never used tobacco products, don't start unless you are willing to deal with the consequences.

For more information on the effects of smoking, visit Ash.org . If you or someone you know is attempting to quit smoking, please visit smokefree.gov for information and help on how to quit.
